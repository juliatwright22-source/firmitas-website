FAQ

Common questions, straight answers.

If your question isn't answered here, the fastest way to get it answered is a discovery conversation.

What is a discovery conversation?

A confidential, no-pressure conversation about your context, your responsibilities, and what you want to build. It is how every Firmitas engagement begins — and it carries no obligation. If Firmitas isn't the right fit, you'll still leave with more clarity than you came with.

Do I need to share your faith to work with Firmitas?

Firmitas coaching is openly anchored to a biblical worldview — that isn't hidden, and it shapes how we think about character, stewardship, and leadership. You don't have to share that conviction to benefit from the coaching, but you should be comfortable with it as the foundation of the counsel you'll receive.

Is this counseling or ministry?

No. Firmitas is executive coaching — a disciplined, confidential process for strengthening how you think, decide, communicate, lead teams, and execute priorities. It is informed by biblical wisdom, but the work is leadership work.

What does a typical engagement look like?

The flagship engagement is one-to-one executive coaching over six months or longer. The rhythm, scope, and priorities are defined together at the start — shaped by your actual responsibilities, not a generic curriculum. Small-team coaching, focused workshops, and speaking engagements are scoped the same way: deliberately, up front.

Is coaching confidential?

Yes. Confidentiality is foundational to the work. In team or sponsored engagements, the confidentiality standards and what a sponsor does and doesn't hear are agreed clearly before coaching begins — so no one is ever guessing.

Who is Firmitas for?

Leaders carrying real responsibility: founders, owners, and CEOs; senior and functional executives; emerging and high-potential leaders; sales and commercial leaders; and small leadership teams of one to four people seeking clarity and alignment together.

What does coaching cost?

Investment depends on the scope of the engagement, and it's discussed openly in the discovery conversation — before any commitment is made. There are no surprises.

Do we need to meet in person?

No. Coaching conversations are held virtually by default, which makes the work accessible wherever you lead. In-person sessions, workshops, and speaking engagements are arranged case by case.
