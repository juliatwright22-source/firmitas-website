Is Firmitas a Christian coaching firm?

Yes. Firmitas is explicitly grounded in biblical principles. Scripture informs our view of leadership, character, stewardship, work, relationships, and legacy. Prospective clients should understand that faith is part of the methodology, not a hidden or optional feature.

Do I need to be a Christian to work with Firmitas?

Firmitas is built for leaders who value or are genuinely open to a biblical worldview. Fit should be discussed during the discovery conversation so expectations are clear on both sides.

Is coaching the same as consulting?

No. Consulting generally provides expert analysis and recommendations. Coaching helps the client examine assumptions, clarify decisions, strengthen leadership capability, and take ownership of action. Firmitas may identify when advisory support is useful, but the two roles should remain clearly defined.

What kinds of leaders do you coach?

Firmitas serves founders, CEOs, senior executives, emerging leaders, sales leaders, and small leadership teams. The firm is not limited to manufacturing or energy, although Jonathan's executive experience in those environments provides practical credibility.

What happens in a discovery conversation?

The conversation explores the leader's context, priorities, desired outcomes, readiness for coaching, and fit with the Firmitas approach. It is intended to create clarity rather than pressure the prospect into an engagement.

How long does an engagement last?

The recommended starting structure is often a six-month engagement, allowing enough time for reflection, application, accountability, and measurable development. The final cadence and term are defined in the coaching agreement.

Is coaching confidential?

Yes, within the boundaries established in the coaching agreement. When a company sponsors the engagement, the parties agree in advance on what may be reported and what remains confidential.
